City of Graham information

Municipal collection info, not a direct booking page

This page stays mostly informational. It tells city customers what to do, when to call City Hall, and where the handoff happens between municipal requests and KC service execution.

City collection basics

Clear answers without sending people in circles

95-gallon polycarts are the standard city containers.
Missed service and cart repair requests should be routed through City Hall work-order channels.
Bulk pickup is shown as the 1st and 3rd Thursday schedule unless otherwise posted.
Holiday schedule changes are posted on the website, Facebook, and through City Hall communication.
Important handoff

When the city office owns the request

City accounts are different from direct rural or roll-off customers. The page makes that obvious so residents do not submit the wrong type of request and clog the office queue.

New city service questions go to City Hall.
Bulk pickup should be scheduled through City Hall.
Hazardous materials stay out of city polycarts.
Practical FAQ

Common city questions

The current live site already has FAQ material. This demo simply makes it easier to scan and keeps the municipal boundary obvious.

What if my trash was missed?

Allow the full service day first, then contact City Hall so a work order can be sent through the correct channel.

How do I order another cart?

Additional city cart requests are routed through City Hall, not through the direct rural service process.

What about recyclables?

The page can point residents to the convenience station and accepted city guidance.

What counts as hazardous material?

Tires, paint, solvents, automotive fluids, batteries, electronics, and similar items should stay out of the cart.
